# Independent Auditor's Reports on DoD FY 2025 Financial Statements (DODIG-2026-032)

This document provides a comprehensive summary of the U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) Financial Statements Audit Report for Fiscal Year 2025, published on December 18, 2025. It details the materiality scale, key audit areas, identified material weaknesses, and insights to assist the DoD in achieving an unmodified (clean) audit opinion by 2028.

---

## 1. Audit Opinion & Materiality (Importance)

The DoD Office of Inspector General (OIG) issued a **Disclaimer of Opinion** on the Agency-Wide Financial Statements. The auditors were unable to obtain sufficient, appropriate evidence to provide a basis for an opinion.

### Materiality Scale
* **Asset & Budget Coverage**: The DoD Components that received disclaimers of opinion represent at least **43% of total DoD assets** and **64% of total DoD budgetary resources**. These balances are highly material to the Consolidated Financial Statements.
* **Large Accounts**:
  * **Fund Balance with Treasury (FBWT)**: $1.0 Trillion.
  * **General Property, Plant, and Equipment (PP&E)**: $501.2 Billion.
  * **Real Property**: $478.7 Billion.
  * **Operating Materials and Supplies (OM&S)**: $231.0 Billion.
  * **Inventory and Stockpile Materials**: $155.4 Billion.
  * **Environmental and Disposal Liabilities (E&DL)**: $108.0 Billion.
  * **Gross Costs**: $1.5 Trillion.
  * **Earned Revenue**: $569.6 Billion.

### Key Audit Findings & Misstatements
* **Building Partner Capacity (BPC) Programs**: A **$18.9 Billion material misstatement** was identified. DoD recorded funds as spent immediately when transferred to a trust fund account rather than when goods/services were actually delivered.
* **Joint Strike Fighter (JSF) Global Spares Pool**: DoD omitted Global Spares Pool assets from the financial statements. This omission resulted in a **material misstatement** of DoD assets, which is **unquantifiable** because DoD cannot verify the spares' existence, completeness, or value.
* **Unsupported Accounting Adjustments**: During the last two quarters of FY 2025 alone, DoD Components recorded **more than 5,665 unsupported adjustments totaling over $859 Billion**.
* **Net Cost Discrepancy**: A **$1.3 Billion unreconcilable difference** was reported between budgetary and proprietary accounts.

## 2. Key Areas & Identified Material Weaknesses

The report details **26 Material Weaknesses** and **2 Significant Deficiencies** in internal control over financial reporting:

### IT Systems & Security Controls (6 Weaknesses)
1. **Financial Management Systems Modernization**: Hundreds of financial systems do not comply with the Federal Financial Management Improvement Act (FFMIA).
2. **Configuration Management**: Component-level financial systems failed to comply with NIST SP 800-53 configuration controls.
3. **Security Management**: Components lacked reviewed policies or Action Plan milestones for known vulnerabilities.
4. **Access Controls**: Failure to review user accounts, maintain logs, or off-board terminated employees.
5. **Segregation of Duties**: Lacked organization-level policies, and failed to enforce least privilege principles.
6. **Interface Controls**: Poor information exchange verification, causing incomplete data transfers.

### Transactions & Accounting (7 Weaknesses)
7. **Universe of Transactions (UoT)**: Inability to provide transaction-level detail supporting adjustments or major line items.
8. **Unsupported Accounting Adjustments**: Large volumes of adjustments recorded without original supporting documentation.
9. **Intragovernmental Transactions**: Lacked documentation for journal vouchers and trading partner confirmations.
10. **Gross Costs**: Lacked support for cost subledgers or accurate cutoff matching.
11. **Earned Revenue**: Poor internal controls over revenue recognition.
12. **Reconciliation of Net Cost to Outlays**: Inability to reconcile Note 24 variances.
13. **Budgetary Resources**: Incomplete and inaccurate SBR, creating Antideficiency Act (ADA) risks.

### Assets & Liabilities (10 Weaknesses)
14. **Fund Balance with Treasury (FBWT)**: TI-97 historical balances could not be verified ($223.8 Billion, 22% of total).
15. **Inventory & Stockpile Materials**: Noncompliant valuation under SFFAS 3 and 48.
16. **Operating Materials & Supplies (OM&S)**: EOU material was not valued at net realizable value.
17. **General Property, Plant, & Equipment (PP&E)**: Lacked documentation to support historical acquisition cost.
18. **Real Property**: Real property records could not be verified for existence.
19. **Government Property in Contractor Possession**: Inability to reconcile contractor-held items.
20. **Joint Strike Fighter Program**: Global spares pool excluded from the balance sheet.
21. **Accounts Payable**: AP not recorded in the correct period.
22. **Environmental & Disposal Liabilities (E&DL)**: Incomplete assessment of asset disposal cleanup costs.
23. **Leases**: Noncompliance with the new lease standard SFFAS 54.

### Monitoring & Oversight (3 Weaknesses)
24. **Service Organizations**: Inadequate monitoring of service agencies (e.g., DFAS), resulting in qualified SOC opinions.
25. **Component Entity-Level Controls**: Control deficiencies at the service-branch level.
26. **DoD-Wide Oversight**: Consolidation data did not match component audited statements.

### Significant Deficiencies (2 Deficiencies)
1. **Risk Management Framework (RMF)**: Incomplete financial system security risk assessments.
2. **Accounts Receivable**: Non-Federal receivables ($15.6B) lacked invoice-level subsidiary ledger reconciliation.

## 3. Road to a Clean Audit Opinion by 2028

In the Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller) response, DoD committed to resolving these critical deficiencies and achieving a clean (unmodified) opinion by 2028.

### Strategic Priorities
1. **Defense Working Capital Fund (DWCF) Milestone (FY 2027)**:
   DoD's immediate focus is preparing a combined DWCF Financial Report to achieve an unmodified opinion on FY 2027 DWCF statements. This serves as the first major building block.
2. **Agency-Wide Financial Report Milestone (FY 2028)**:
   DoD will scale the lessons from the DWCF audit to achieve a clean opinion on the FY 2028 Consolidated Agency-Wide Financial Statements.
3. **AI & Automation Integration**:
   DoD is deploying AI algorithms to automate internal control activities, monitor ledgers for anomalies, reconcile transactions in real-time, and eliminate manual data-entry errors.
4. **Timeline Reconciliation**:
   Audit reports highlight that some general ledger systems are not scheduled to retire until FY 2031. To hit the 2028 goal, DoD must expedite legacy systems retirement and bring compliance timelines forward.
5. **Universe of Transactions (UoT)**:
   Transitioning from summary-level reconciliations to transaction-level audits will verify the completeness of general ledger populations, resolving the unsupported adjustment issues.
